My Statistically Rigged Casino Games

The statistical concepts I used in this activity were expected value and probability distribution. Probability distribution is maps out every possible outcome of a random event (the net gain or loss for the player) and assigns a precise probability to each outcome. The tables in the document illustrate this by showing the two possible results (win/loss), the likelihood of each, and the monetary value assigned. Expected Value is the long-run average outcome per trial if the game were played an infinite number of times. It is calculated as the sum of all possible outcomes multiplied by their respective probabilities (a weighted average). A positive EV (Game A) means the player is theoretically guaranteed to profit over time, while a negative EV (Game B) guarantees the casino a long-term profit. It is safe to assume that when repeated over multiple iterations, it will eventually lead to the expected outcome by using the Law of large numbers. The law of large numbers is an important theorem in probability that states as the number of independent trials increases, the average result of the trials will converge to the true Expected Value. Game A (Positive Expected Value)

Outcome: Player Always Wins (Profit)

This game is played with a standard 52-card deck. The player pays $1.00 to play and bets on whether the drawn card is a Prime Number Card (2, 3, 5, or 7). Face cards (J, Q, K, A) are ignored and result in a redraw. The game is designed to guarantee a positive expected value for the player.

The rules are summarized below:

  • Cost to play: $1.00.
  • Play: Draw 1 card from a shuffled 52-card deck.
  • Win condition: Card is a Prime Number (2, 3, 5, 7, 16 total cards).
  • Payout: Win $3.50 (net + $2.50).
  • Loss condition: Card is a Composite Number (4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 20 total cards).
  • Loss: Win nothing (net - $1.00).

Probability Distribution

The probability of drawing a Prime card is 16/52, or approximately 0.3077. The probability of drawing a Composite card is 36/52, or approximately 0.6923.

Outcome Probability P(X) Payout ($) Net Gain ($)
Prime Card 0.3077 3.50 +2.50
Composite Card 0.6923 0.00 -1.00

Expected Value Calculation

From this distribution, we calculate the expected value E: E = (+2.50 * 0.3077) + (-1.00 * 0.6923). This calculates to approximately +0.7692 - 0.6923, which is +0.0769. This equals about + $0.08, meaning on average the player gains about 8 cents per play.

Game B (Negative Expected Value)

Outcome: Casino Always Wins (Profit)

This game is played with a six-sided die that is secretly weighted to land on '1' 20% of the time (instead of the fair 16.67%). The remaining sides (2, 3, 4, 5, 6) each have a 16% probability. The player pays $1.00$ and bets on rolling a High Number (4, 5, 6).

The rules are summarized below:

  • Cost to play: $1.00.
  • Play: Roll the weighted 6-sided die.
  • Win condition: Roll a High Number (4, 5, 6).
  • Payout: Win $2.00 (net + $1.00).
  • Loss condition: Roll a Low Number (1, 2, 3).
  • Loss: Win nothing (net - $1.00).

Probability Distribution

The probability of rolling High is P(4) + P(5) + P(6) = 3 * 0.16 = 0.48. The probability of rolling Low is P(1) + P(2) + P(3) = 0.20 + 0.16 + 0.16 = 0.52.

Outcome Probability P(X) Payout ($) Net Gain ($)
High (4, 5, 6) 0.48 2.00 +1.00
Low (1, 2, 3) 0.52 0.00 -1.00

Expected Value Calculation

From this distribution, we calculate the expected value E: E = (+1.00 * 0.48) + (-1.00 * 0.52). This calculates to 0.48 - 0.52 = -0.04. This equals exactly - $0.04, meaning on average the player loses about 4 cents per play (The Casino's Profit).
